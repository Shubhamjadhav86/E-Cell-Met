Executive Summary

On November 27, 2025, MET AMDC and IIBHM hosted TECHXPO 2025, a flagship "Innovation-to-Market" platform. The expo was designed to transform academic research—specifically Final-Year Projects—into commercially viable startups through industry benchmarking and capital infusion.

Academia-to-Startup Framework

The event focused on the Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of each project, guiding students through a structured validation funnel:

  • Research Validation: Defending technical soundness.
  • Market Viability: Identifying pain points addressed by the tech.
  • MVP Prototyping: Live demonstrations of working products.
  • Investor Pitching: Securing seed grants for commercialization.

The Investor Jury Round

Promising exhibitors faced a closed-door session led by Bhavesh Salunke and Samir Shah. Evaluation focused on Intellectual Property (IP), Cost of Acquisition, and Multidisciplinary Team Capability.

Impact & Conclusion

TECHXPO 2025 has successfully shifted the student perception of final-year projects from an academic requirement to a potential career path. Several teams have already entered post-event mentorship for patent filing and small-scale manufacturing.
