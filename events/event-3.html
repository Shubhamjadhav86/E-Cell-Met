IIC IPL Mock Auction

EXPERIENTIAL LEARNING • STRATEGIC MANAGEMENT SIMULATION
+0

Executive Summary

On December 1, 2024, the E-Cell, in collaboration with the Institution’s Innovation Council (IIC), organized the IIC IPL Mock Auction. This high-fidelity simulation served as a pedagogical tool for teaching strategic negotiation, budget management, and data-driven decision-making.

Participants operated within a "Closed-System Market," where student teams managed virtual capital to build competitive rosters. The team representing Rajasthan Royals emerged as winners, demonstrating superior financial discipline and roster balancing.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Asset Valuation: Determining intrinsic value vs. market-driven prices.
  • Budgetary Constraint: Strategic allocation of finite virtual cash.
  • Risk Mitigation: Using data analytics to avoid the "Winner's Curse" (overpaying).
  • Team Dynamics: Real-world corporate roles: Negotiators, Financial Analysts, and Data Analysts.

Operational Strategies Observed

The venue was transformed into a "War Room" environment. Teams utilized specialized Auction Management Software and manual logs to execute strategies such as Shadow Bidding (driving up rival prices) and the Wait-and-Watch strategy (saving capital for high-value uncapped players).

Event Details
